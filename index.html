How to Play Texas Hold'em

Each player is dealt two private hole cards. Five community cards are revealed face-up across three rounds — the flop, the turn and the river. You make your best five-card hand from any combination of your two hole cards and the five shared cards. Betting takes place over four rounds (pre-flop, flop, turn and river), and on your turn you can fold, check or call, or raise. The forced small and big blinds start each pot and increase over time. The last player standing, or the best hand at showdown, wins the pot.

How to Play Texas Hold'em

Betting happens in four rounds — pre-flop, flop, turn and river. On your turn you can Fold, Check/Call, or Raise. The small and big blinds are forced bets that start the pot, and they rise over time. Last player standing — or the best hand at showdown — wins the pot.
